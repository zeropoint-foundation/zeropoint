From the Author

More than a year ago, I set out to build the SSL/TLS we were going to need for the Agentic Age.

Not because someone asked me to. Because I could see we were starting to lose trust in a lot of what we used to rely on. In institutions, in platforms, in the systems we'd built to hold things together. And I could see what was coming next — the agents, the autonomy, the delegation of real decisions to systems that wouldn't be able to explain themselves. Trust was already fraying. Autonomy without accountability was going to finish the job.

I knew that if we didn't start building alternatives to the enshittification we can already see, there just wouldn't be any. Not because I had it all figured out or was even sure I could affect the calculus of capture — but because doing nothing was not an option I could live with.

So I built ZeroPoint. With love and intention, and sometimes with the felt weight of the world upon my shoulders. I knew trust needed to be portable. I knew receipts needed to chain. I knew authority needed to narrow as it delegated. And I had the cryptography and the design instinct to start building. What I didn't have was the language for why these patterns felt necessary rather than merely useful.

The language came from an unexpected direction. Researchers studying language models, cognition, and even physics are converging on the same computational pattern: the present carries the full weight of the past, and coherent futures emerge from locally conditioned steps. They call it autoregression. I call it trust as trajectory.

ZeroPoint didn't adopt this theory. It converged on it independently — because this is how trust actually works. Not as a score to be checked, but as a path to be walked and verified. The architecture came first. Then came the language to understand why it matters. And with that understanding came something I hadn't expected: the conviction that this wasn't just useful engineering. It was a structural requirement. Trust that doesn't unfold — that can't be traced, can't be verified, can't be revoked — isn't trust at all. It's hope dressed up as infrastructure.

That conviction is what carries the work forward now. ZeroPoint is a foundation — literally, as of this year — because the tools we build today decide the civilization we get tomorrow. And because I believe, stubbornly and without apology, that we can still choose better.

This isn't a product pitch. It's a bet on us — as a species. On the idea that the right infrastructure, built early enough, changes what's possible — and that we're still early enough.

Ken Romero
Founder, ThinkStream Labs
May 2026
