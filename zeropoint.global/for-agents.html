Governing Agents

You deploy the agents.
ZeroPoint makes them accountable.

Agents don't choose governance — you do. ZeroPoint installs into your stack and wraps every interface with cryptographic proof. Every action gets a signed receipt. Every decision gets an audit trail. Your agents don't need to know it's there. They just become governable.

The question isn't whether to trust your agents. It's whether you can prove what they did.

The Problem

Agents are powerful. They're also opaque.

You're deploying agents that call tools, make decisions, and produce artifacts — often across multiple runtimes, orchestrators, and model providers. When something goes wrong, you get logs if you're lucky. You get nothing if you're not.

Humans build trust through implicit trajectory — years of interaction, social context, reputation built in relationships. Agents don't have that luxury. They operate at speed, in isolation, with no historical relationship to vouch for them. Trust isn't implicit; it must be explicit.

The harder question: when things go right, can you prove it? Can you show an auditor, a client, or a regulator exactly what happened, in what order, with what authority? Without an explicit trajectory of evidence, agent autonomy is a liability.

How It Works

Install once. Govern transparently.

ZeroPoint doesn't require your agents to integrate anything. It wraps the interfaces they already use. Every action becomes a link in an explicit trust trajectory.

01

Install & Configure

Deploy ZeroPoint into your environment. Define policies — what actions are allowed, what requires review, what gets blocked. Your rules, cryptographically enforced.

02

Wrap & Guard

ZeroPoint's guard evaluates every action against your policy set. Each evaluation produces a signed receipt: action, decision, rationale, timestamp, trust grade. The agent doesn't need to cooperate.

03

Verify & Prove

Receipts chain together with hash links. Verify the chain at any time. Hand it to an auditor. Query it programmatically. The proof is always there, always portable, always yours.

The Output

What a governed agent produces

Every action through ZeroPoint generates a receipt. This is what one looks like.

A ZeroPoint Receipt

Each receipt contains: a unique ID, a content hash (Blake3), an Ed25519 signature, a link to the parent receipt, the action evaluated, the policy decision and rationale, a trust grade, and a timestamp. Chained together, these form a tamper-evident audit trail.

In-Band Proof

proof: zp:receipt:<receipt_id>

Agents can attach this header to any output. Any system receiving it can fetch the receipt, verify the chain, and decide how much to trust the claim. The proof travels with the work.
