# Governance

ZeroPoint is a project about governance — so how the project itself is governed matters.

## Decision-Making

ZeroPoint is currently maintained by [Ken Romero](mailto:ken@thinkstreamlabs.ai) at ThinkStream Labs. As the project grows, governance will evolve toward broader community participation while maintaining constitutional consistency.

All technical decisions are guided by the Four Tenets:

1. **Do No Harm** — No feature, optimization, or integration justifies enabling surveillance, coercion, or violation of autonomy.
2. **Respect Sovereignty** — Participants control their own data, identity, and participation. This extends to contributors controlling their own work.
3. **Integrity First** — Every decision and its reasoning should be traceable. We don't merge code we can't explain.
4. **Transparency** — Governance processes, roadmap priorities, and architectural decisions are public.

## What Gets Merged

Pull requests are evaluated on four criteria:

- **Constitutional alignment** — Does it strengthen or weaken the Four Tenets?
- **Architectural coherence** — Does it respect crate boundaries and the governance pipeline?
- **Code quality** — Does it pass CI (build, test, clippy, fmt) with zero warnings?
- **Clear purpose** — Can the contributor explain what it does and why it matters?

Contributions that weaken constitutional guarantees, enable surveillance, or bypass governance checks will not be accepted regardless of technical quality.

## Roadmap

The current roadmap is maintained in the [whitepaper](https://zeropoint.global/whitepaper) and discussed in GitHub Issues. Major architectural changes are proposed through Issues before implementation.

## Becoming a Maintainer

As the community grows, maintainers will be invited based on sustained, high-quality contributions and demonstrated alignment with the Four Tenets. Maintainership is a responsibility, not a reward.

## Dispute Resolution

Technical disagreements are resolved through discussion on the relevant Issue or PR. If consensus cannot be reached, the project lead makes the final call with a written explanation.

Constitutional disputes — questions about whether a change violates the Four Tenets — are treated with extra care. These decisions are documented publicly and can be revisited as understanding evolves.
