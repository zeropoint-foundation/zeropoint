03 — your vault

Understanding Your Vault

Right now, your API keys probably live in .env files, password managers, or cloud secrets services — all mediated by someone else's infrastructure. ZeroPoint replaces that. It derives a vault key from your Genesis secret and encrypts every credential you store with ChaCha20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. No cloud. No intermediary.

Genesis Secret (in credential store) | v BLAKE3-keyed("zp-credential-vault-v1") | v Vault Key (ephemeral — exists only in memory) | v ChaCha20-Poly1305 → Your encrypted credentials

You don't manage the vault key. It's derived on demand from your Genesis secret, used, then wiped from memory. There is nothing to remember, back up, or rotate.

what you can change later

zp vault import — restore on new machine
