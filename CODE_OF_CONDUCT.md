# Code of Conduct

ZeroPoint is a trust project. The community that builds it must be trustworthy.

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and safe environment for all community members. ZeroPoint's foundation is the Four Tenets—principles that demand respect, integrity, and honesty in all we do. This code of conduct applies those principles to how we treat each other.

## Core Expectations

### Respect
Treat all contributors, maintainers, and community members with dignity. This means:
- Listen to different perspectives and backgrounds
- Value disagreement as an opportunity to strengthen our work
- Critique ideas, not people
- No harassment, discrimination, or hostility

### Good Faith
Assume good intentions. This means:
- Engage thoughtfully in discussions
- Ask clarifying questions before assuming malice
- Acknowledge when you've misunderstood someone
- Work toward resolution, not victory

### Intellectual Honesty
The integrity principle demands honesty in our work:
- Acknowledge limitations in your knowledge or code
- Cite sources and credit contributors
- Report bugs and security issues accurately
- Don't misrepresent the capabilities or safety of ZeroPoint

### No Surveillance Advocacy
Do No Harm means we will never serve surveillance:
- Don't advocate for or propose surveillance capabilities
- Don't pressure others to weaken privacy protections
- Don't weaponize ZeroPoint or its infrastructure
- Call this out if you see it

## The Four Tenets as Community Standards

1. **Do No Harm**: Treat each other with the same respect we demand from our systems. Exclusion, dismissal, and psychological harm are all forms of harm.

2. **Respect Sovereignty**: Honor each person's autonomy. Different viewpoints, approaches, and needs matter. We don't coerce participation or agreement.

3. **Integrity First**: Everything is verifiable. Our discussions should be honest, evidence-based, and transparent. Deception has no place here.

4. **Transparency**: We explain our decisions, reasoning, and concerns clearly. Secrecy breeds distrust; transparency builds it.

## Reporting

If you witness or experience a violation of this code of conduct, please report it to:

**ken@thinkstreamlabs.ai**

Include:
- A description of what happened
- When it occurred
- Who was involved (if you're comfortable sharing)
- Any context that helps us understand

Reports are treated confidentially. We will acknowledge receipt within 48 hours.

## Enforcement

Our response depends on the severity and pattern:

1. **First incident (minor)**: A private conversation explaining the concern and why the behavior conflicts with our values. We assume good faith and offer an opportunity to understand.

2. **Repeated behavior or significant harm**: A formal warning. We'll explain what needs to change and set a timeframe.

3. **Refusal to change or severe violation**: Temporary or permanent removal from the community. We will explain our reasoning clearly.

**We always explain our decisions.** Enforcement without explanation breeds the distrust we're trying to prevent.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:
- All interactions in GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions)
- Email and direct communication with maintainers
- Any official ZeroPoint community spaces (forums, chat, etc.)
- Conference talks and community events

## Questions?

If you're uncertain whether something violates this code, ask. We'd rather clarify expectations than enforce ambiguity.
